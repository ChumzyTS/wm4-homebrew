**Bathaerel**  
*Adventuring Gear, Consumable*  
*15 gp, 1 lb*

Also known as "rustbanish", Bathaerel is a purplish powder or gravel made of certain rocks from the Abyssal layers of Rauwend and Voorz'zt. When the rock is agitated in a bag with rusty metal items or mixed into a paste with water, crushed raw potato or grist, and certain green-shoot plants, the resulting mixture stops rust and removes it, leaving behind clean metal. However, it doesn't replace metal already lost to rust.

One pound of this paste can coat one item. Applying the paste takes 1 minute. All rust on the item is removed, and for one hour, the item cannot rust, including from rusting effects like Rust Monsters produce.

*From S3 & Dragon Magazine \#421 in an article by Ed Greenwood; Ported by Aris*

**Droth**  
*Adventuring Gear, Consumable*  
*75 gp*

Also called "demon's blood", Droth is a black, sticky substance made from the blood of Manes and the boiled down chitin of Dhergoloths. When smeared on the eyes, it cures certain sorts of blindness in some individuals, and when ingested (its taste is generally described as saldy and horrible), it can help to cure certain contagions. When smeared on acid-drenched items or living creatures, it cuts the corrosion done by the acid in half. Ingested or smeared, droth stops all green slime effects instantly, and turns back existing slime into the living material it was before being slimed.

Ingesting or smearing Droth takes a Utilize action. When you smear Droth on your eyes, you gain Advantage on saving throws to end the Blinded condition for 1 hour. When you ingest Droth, you have Advantage on saving throws made to end the effects of a Magical Contagion for 1 hour.

Droth can be applied to a creature or object with acid on it as a Utilize action. The effect causing the acid will deal half damage until it is removed. In addition, if a patch of green slime touches an object or creature with Droth applied, or Droth is applied to a patch of green slime, the slime effect ends instantly, and it reverts to the living material it was before being slimed.

*From S3 & Dragon Magazine \#421 in an article by Ed Greenwood, ported by Aris*

**Moonhoney**  
*Adventuring gear, consumable*  
*100 gp, 1 lb*

The substance with the unlikely name of moonhoney is actually the dung of ground-worms that dwell on many Abyssal layers (and which are eaten by many demons). It is a smokey-tasting, nourishing, even rib-sticking treat to most humanoids. Its name comes from its consistency and appearance (both of which are rather like the honey produced by wild bees), and the fact that when bathed in moonlight, it momentarily acquires a rough, fleeting sweetness. Moonhoney doesn't spoil unless it is scorched in open flame or soaked in citrus juices, and so it is an ideal trail food for wayfarers of all kinds, who can readily carve into handy chunks. It is prized for its property of neutralizing almost all known poisons active within those who eat it.

Consuming a chunk of moonhoney rids you of the poisoned condition.

*From S3 & Dragon Magazine \#421 in an article by Ed Greenwood, Ported by Aris*

**Traveler's Dust**  
*Drug*

Traveler's dust is transported and sold in the form of tiny roseate crystals, each a bit bigger than a grain of salt. Traveler's dust is used by dropping a single grain of the drug onto the eye and letting it dissolve.

When you dissolve a crystal in your eye, your vision becomes hazy with red, and you see a breathtaking vista lining a winding, Crimson Road. This vision is from your astral body, as your soul makes a journey.

While your astral form strides upon the Crimson Road (you are compelled to, and cannot resist the urge), all sorrows sink beyond recall, while all joys are raised like blazing stars. You feel alive, powerful, changed.

One trip of the drug lasts an hour, though more consumption can continually prolong the experience. At the end of every hour while under the effects of this drug, make a DC 20 Charisma saving throw. Once you reach 5 failures from this drug, your astral body crosses the precipice of the tooth-lined pit at the end of the Crimson Road. The soul falls into the Abyss, where your mind and soul are devoured by awaiting demons. You die, and your soul is destroyed. You can only be revived by a True Resurrection or Wish spell.

While travelling, you are only vaguely aware of your actual surroundings. Your hands tremble, your speech is slurred, and your eyes are the color of blood.

Resuming the drug at any point after a trip causes your astral body to resume from where it left off.

The drug is incredibly addictive, and if you are deprived of the substance for more than eight hours after having experienced the drug, you become violent, first towards others around you, and then to yourself.

GMs should not give this drug out lightly, and should be careful that the player is okay with receiving the drug, and that portrayals of the experience are sufficiently content warned.

*From S3; By Aris*

**Caligrostro Blade**  
*Weapon (Any Piercing or Slashing Weapon), Legacy (Requires Attunement)*

Caligrostro blades are not like other Legacy items, and many such items exist. They result from a Caligrostro demon possessing a magic weapon, changing and warping it with demonic power. Casting Dispel Evil and Good on the blade forces the demon to leave it. The blade then becomes a \+1 weapon with no other properties.

The blade can choose to instantly attune to a creature it touches, ending any current attunements to it.

You have a \+1 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with this magic weapon. 

**Brutal Feast (Level 3).** When you kill a creature with this weapon, you gain 10 Temporary Hit Points. Alternatively, you can feed one of the creature's Hit Die to the blade.

**Keen (Level 6).** Attacks with this weapon score a Critical Hit on a roll of 19 or 20 on the d20.

**Fiendish Shade (Level 6).** The Caligrostro can expend 6 Hit Dice that it has consumed, it creates a sword wraith under its control in an unoccupied space within 5 feet, and the blade it is possessing flies into the sword wraith's grasp and immediately attunes to the sword wraith (but it keeps its hit dice).

The sword wraith has the Fiend (Demon; Loumara) creature type, and it resembles its previous wielder but a more fiendish version. It also has a \+2 bonus to attack rolls against its previous owner. It is Resistant to all damage except Poison damage (which it is Immune to), and any damage it takes, its previous wielder also takes.

While wielded by the Sword Wraith, the blade has the benefits of all its Hit Dice. Once slain, the blade acts under its wielder’s control for the next 24 hours.

**Demonic Strikes (Level 9).** Attacks with this weapon deal an extra 1d10 Necrotic damage on a hit. On a hit, you can choose to expend 1 Hit Die of the blade and roll it, and the target takes extra Necrotic damage equal to the number rolled.

**Black Possession (Level 12).** While Incapacitated, you ignore the effects of being Incapacitated and act under the blade's control.

**Dance Partner (Level 15).** As a Bonus Action you can release the blade. When you do so, the weapon begins to hover, flies up to 30 feet, and attacks one creature of your choice within 5 feet of itself. The weapon uses your attack roll and adds your ability modifier to damage rolls.

**Abyssal Doom (Level 18).** The weapon now ignores Resistance to Necrotic damage. When you use this weapon to attack a creature and roll a 19 or 20 on the d20 for the attack roll, you can instantly slay the creature and send its soul to the Abyss. A creature is immune to this effect if it has Immunity to Necrotic damage, lacks a soul, or is a Construct, Ooze, or Undead. Such a creature instead takes an extra 30 Necrotic damage from the hit. If the creature has Legendary Resistance, it can expend one daily use of that trait to avoid dying, taking the extra damage instead.

**Legacy Rituals.** This weapon feeds on the lifeforce of its wielder, empowering the blade. Only one ritual is needed to unlock the abilities of the Caligrostro Blade, though two exist. The Blade’s features are only unlocked if the Caligrostro blade is fed a number of Hit Dice equal to the Level prerequisite. The blade loses Hit Dice when the attuned creature finishes a long rest, or the creature becomes unattuned. Certain features also cause the blade to lose hit dice.

* **Bloodfed Blade:** As a Magic action, you may choose to lose up to three unexpended Hit Dice, feeding them to the blade.

* **Lifeblood Thief:** At any time but no more than once per round, the blade can choose to force you to make a DC 15 Constitution saving throw. On a failure, you must expend 1 Hit Die and roll it, and you take Necrotic damage equal to the number rolled. The blade gains 1 Hit Die. If you have no Hit Die to spend, you drop to 0 Hit Points.

**Curse.** While attuned to this weapon, you have Disadvantage on attack rolls made with weapons other than this one, and you can't willingly end your attunement to it. The Caligrostro is Chaotic Evil and has its own personality. It can telepathically communicate with creatures within 120 feet of it, including its wielder. It seeks to be fed, and will forcibly drain Hit Dice if it is not fed by its wielder. It will gladly betray its wielder for another stronger creature if it thinks it can be fed more, but shows of strength can impress it, and it can be won over. Some partnerships between wielder and Caligrostro have been known to form. The demon has 16 Int, 15 Wis, 19 Cha, 120 feet Darkvision, Chaotic Evil, and can speak Common and Abyssal. It cannot be detected by Divination magic.

*From S3, Made by Aris*

**Facade**  
*Wonderous Item, Legacy (Requires Attunement by a Spellcaster)*

The amulet appears however you choose, and you can change its appearance freely. It can be used as a spellcasting focus for your spells.

**Manifestation (Level 6).** As a Bonus Action, you can force any creatures of your choice within the area of an Illusion of yours to make a Wisdom saving throw. On a failure, a target is either Frightened or Poisoned (your choice) until the end of your next turn, or until it leaves the area of the Illusion. This only affects creatures that can perceive the Illusion.

**Resonant Illusions (Level 7).** You can create illusory sounds to accompany any illusions you create. The sound cannot be loud enough to deal Thunder damage or Deafen a creature. You can choose for Tremorsense to detect your Illusions as if they were real. In addition, the Deafened condition is added as an option for Manifestation.

**Far Fetched (Level 8).** The range of your Illusion spells are doubled.

**Blind Spot (Level 10).** You can choose for Blindsight to detect your Illusions as if they were real. In addition, the Blinded condition is added as an option for Manifestation.

**Noble Phantasm (Level 13).** As a Bonus Action, or as part of the Bonus Action you use for Manifestation, you can deal 4d6 Psychic damage to each creature you choose that you are targeting with an Illusion or that are within the area of an Illusion of yours or within 5 feet of one, provided that the Illusion is of a creature or hazard that could logically deal damage, such as by attacking. The target perceives the damage as a type appropriate to the Illusion. Creatures that cannot perceive the Illusion are unaffected.  
    
**Truth be Told (Level 15).** Physical interaction with your Illusions of your choice does not reveal them to be Illusions. You can choose for Truesight to detect your Illusions as if they were real, and creatures with Truesight do not automatically save against your Illusions. In addition, the Charmed condition is added as an option for Manifestation.

**Shadowshaper (Level 17).** You can use spell slots of any level to cast the Creation spell, not just Level 5 or greater slots, and you can cast the spell as a Magic action (instead of its normal casting time). In addition, the Grappled and Restrained conditions are added as options for Manifestation.

**The Prestige (Level 21).** When one of your Illusion spells ends, you can use your Reaction to choose one of the following effects:  
All creatures within 5 feet of the illusion that ends must make a Wisdom saving throw. On a failure, the creature is either Charmed, Confused, Dazed, Asleep, Frightened, Blinded, or takes 5d10 Psychic damage (your choice). If you choose a condition, it lasts until the end of the creature's next turn.  
You can immediately cast another Illusion spell, but the spell must be of a higher level than the Illusion that ended, and have a casting time of 1 action.

**Legacy Rituals.** These three rituals must be completed to unlock Facade's Legacy features.

* **Utilitarian Fabrication:** Use an illusion to save someone from grave danger, without bringing harm to anyone else. This unlocks its level 6-10 features.

* **Hedonistic Fabrication:** Use an illusion to maximize your happiness and wellbeing to the utmost, perhaps even endangering others gravely. This unlocks the level 13 feature.

* **Act Utilitarianism:** Complete an extremely deadly encounter with the caveat of only using illusory magic. You may only use spells of the Illusion school of magic for this combat. This unlocks it's level 15-21 features.
